Inspirations


Pitch black theatre

The audience enjoys the play in complete darkness.

On the one hand, being in a pitch black environment terrifies the ancestral part of us with the threat of unknowns. On the other hand, we snuggle into the total darkness because how often do we city dwellers get to disappear into darkness and not get speculated?

With our eyes open, the world is heavily visual-dependent. The location of consciousness sits right at our forehead. With our eyes closed, the location of consciousness sinks to the other parts of the body. The senses of hearing, smelling, touching get magnified tremendously.

Performance for one audience

I first learned of this concept in Professor Craig Quintero ’s class. Each show is designed for just an audience of one. The plot is reactive to the audience at every moment. Between the audience and the actors, it is no longer clear who is on stage and who is not, who watches and who is being watched. Maybe as the audience, you are in the play from the very beginning, just that we forget to hand over your lines.

Immersive Theatre

Conventional plays take place in the theatre. But in immersive plays, the stage can be anywhere around you. The most famous immersive play so far, Sleep no more, takes place in a modified five-storey hotel in New York City. <梦游症-Sleepwalker> will take place on Valencia Street of San Fransisco.







梦游症 - Sleepwalker is a site-specific play.

For an audience of one.

With no visuals.*
